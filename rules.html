Rules & Regulations

Whether you are a seasoned competitor or stepping onto our courts for the first time, mastering the essential boundaries and regulations keeps the spirit of the game clean, fair, and incredibly engaging.

Core Regulations & Scoring

The Best of Three

A standard match consists of the best of three games. The first side to score 21 points wins a game, and a match concludes as soon as one side secures two game victories.

Rally Scoring

Whichever side wins a rally adds a point to its score. You do not need to be the serving side to score a point. The winning side of a rally also earns the right to serve next.

Setting the Margin

If the score reaches 20-all, the side which gains a 2-point lead first wins that game. If the battle continues all the way to 29-all, the side scoring the 30th point wins the game immediately.

The Service Protocol

When serving, the shuttlecock must be struck completely below waist height (strictly measured as 1.15 meters from the ground), and the server's racket shaft must point downwards at the moment of contact.

Official Badminton Events

Badminton features highly diverse event dynamic models that transform the tactical pace and spatial mechanics of play:

  • Singles (Men's / Women's): A pure test of stamina, mental focus, and court coverage. The boundary lines extend long but stay narrow, completely ignoring the side tramlines. Service alternates from the right side when the server's score is even, and from the left side when the score is odd.
  • Doubles (Men's / Women's / Mixed): A high-velocity event requiring rapid synchronization and fluid rotations. The entire court is utilized, meaning the wider side tramlines are fully active during rallies. However, during the initial serve phase, the back boundary court line is shorter.
